XR -

Extended reality refers to all real-and-virtual combined environments, where the 'x' represents a variable for any current or future spatial computing technologies.

If we think about it backwards, XR, unlike AR or VR, is not bound to any particular environment (whether digital or physical, It is blend of both, kinda like Iron Man's hologram... shout out Tony Stark.

AR -

Augmented Reality (AR) is where content lays over users' real-world views. The technology allows people to add digital assets to their physical environment. Sounds crazy, techy and weired but if you've ever used that dog filter on Snapchat, you've joined the AR club.*

* This isn't a real club but if it was, who do you know here?

VR -

Virtual Reality (VR): The thing that'll make your grandma dizzy and your kid feel like superman. Virtual Reality (VR) is the use of computer technology to create a simulated environment. VR places the user inside an experience. Instesd of viewing a screen in front of them, users are immersed and able to interact with 3D worlds (with a headset like Oculus or Vive).
